# Code Of Conduct

SpeakRight Desktop is a pronunciation-learning project. Discussions can touch
accent, language identity, learner confidence, recordings, and source evidence,
so contributors are expected to keep the project respectful and evidence-first.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind to learners and contributors. Correct mistakes without mocking
  accents, first languages, regions, or skill levels.
- Discuss pronunciation, IPA, audio, and scoring changes with evidence. For
  Spanish, French, and Russian, keep `needs-review` rows unchanged until there
  is stronger source support.
- Keep release testing honest. Use the Release EXE for user-facing desktop
  validation and do not describe Spanish, French, or Russian as mastered while
  they remain experimental.
- Protect privacy. Do not post API keys, tokens, private recordings, learning
  data exports, or screenshots that reveal credentials or personal data.
- Respect third-party media boundaries. Do not add or redistribute audio,
  video, images, or voice assets unless their source and allowed use are clear.
- Avoid unapproved quota use. Do not generate ElevenLabs audio or spend TTS
  credits unless a maintainer has explicitly approved that exact pass.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, slurs, personal attacks, or repeated hostile comments.
- Dismissing pronunciation concerns because of a learner's accent, region, or
  first language.
- Posting secrets, raw private recordings, private user data, or vulnerability
  details in public issues.
- Presenting unsourced IPA changes, proxy media, or experimental-language
  results as settled product truth.
- Pressuring contributors to run paid providers, generate TTS, or expose
  private credentials.

## Reporting

For conduct concerns, contact the repository owner through GitHub. For
vulnerabilities, leaked credentials, or private user data, follow
`SECURITY.md` instead of opening a public issue.

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, or
other contributions that violate this code. Repeated or severe violations may
lead to temporary or permanent loss of participation privileges.
